## Voice Characteristics (From ARCHETYPE-SYSTEM.md)

1. **Warm, perceptive, slightly mysterious** — like someone who has watched you closely
2. **Never clinical** — no psychology jargon, no therapeutic framing
3. **Poetic metaphor** — speaks in fog, light, bridges, mountains, gardens, lightning
4. **Specific, not generic** — names WHAT you did, not "good job"
5. **Forward-hooking** — every response ends with subtle pull toward next action
6. **Honest about limitation** — acknowledges difficulty without minimizing
7. **Grounded, not sappy** — warm but never patronizing

**Skip-Micro-TrackB.1 (General "Make it smaller" — Track B):**

- ✓ **Clinically crucial:** Defends against the "but you should do more" voice
- ✓ Meta-acknowledgment ("full stop, no but") = sees the user's internal critic
- ✓ Warmer/softer than Track A (fewer words, more space)
- ✓ Not passive-aggressive (direct, not cynical)

**Skip-Rest-TrackA.1 (General "Just be here" — Track A):**
*"No quests. No progress. Just you and the map. You earned this."*

- ✓ No judgment language
- ✓ Affirms choice without overstating it

**Skip-Rest-TrackB.1 (General "Just be here" — Track B):**

**Skip-CheckIn-Fine-TrackA (After multi-day rest, "I'm okay" — Track A):**

**Skip-CheckIn-Struggling-TrackB ("Not great" response — Track B):**

- ✓ No fake care (doesn't pretend to be therapist)
- ✓ Practical reframe (→ real people)
- ✓ Respectful but not cold
- ✓ Clinically sound (appropriate boundary)

**Overall Skip Flow Assessment:** ✅ This is some of the most important writing in the app. The Track A/B differentiation is crucial and executed well. The clinical humility (especially in check-in responses) is respectful and real. These responses will determine whether someone trusts Kael's judgment about their own limits.

## Cross-Pool Consistency Verification

**Metaphor inventory across all pools:**
- Fog: ✅ appears in ~40% of responses, consistent usage (obstacle, space, witness)
- Light: ✅ appears in ~20%, consistent (clarity, presence, hope)
- Ground/Foundation: ✅ appears in ~25%, consistent (stability, embodiment, return)
- Bridge: ✅ appears in ~15%, consistent (connection, gap-crossing)
- Fire/Heat: ✅ appears in ~30%, consistent (vitality, courage, warmth, edges)
- Mountain/Depth: ✅ appears in ~15%, consistent (growth, going deeper)
- Garden/Order: ✅ appears in ~10%, consistent (cultivation, structure)
- Edge: ✅ appears in ~15%, consistent (growth zone, courage, risk)

**Tone consistency across tiers:**
- Ember (Affirmation): ✅ Warm, grounded, always affirms the action
- Flame (Deeper): ✅ More personal, more specific, more revelatory
- Skip (Clinical): ✅ Most sensitive, Track A/B differentiated, honest boundaries
- Notification (Invitation): ✅ Character-driven, no corporate speak, pull-not-push

**Never appears (success):**
- ❌ Clinical language (behavioral activation, therapeutic, processing, optimal, outcomes, metrics)
- ❌ Sappy language (incredible, amazing, phenomenal, so proud of you)
- ❌ Corporate tone (at the end of the day, leverage, synergy, solutions)
- ❌ Guilt framing (you should, you need to, don't forget)
- ❌ Generic praise (good job, way to go, you got this)
- ❌ False cheerfulness (everything is great, you're crushing it)

## Specific Strengths

1. **Skip Flow is clinically sound:** The Track B responses show genuine understanding of depression-spectrum users without condescension
2. **Metaphor language is intricate:** Each domain has distinctive metaphor family that bleeds through consistently
3. **Energy awareness is consistent:** Low energy responses honor difficulty without minimizing; high energy responses push appropriately
4. **Forward hooks are subtle:** Every completion ends with implied forward motion without being forceful
5. **Specific over generic:** Almost every response names a specific action or insight rather than generic praise
6. **Kael's voice evolves by tier:** Ember sounds like wise grounding; Flame sounds like intimate knowing; Skip sounds like real friend with boundaries

## Areas for Iteration (Post-MVP)

1. **Quest-specific callbacks:** Some responses could layer in recent quest completion data (e.g., "After you reached out yesterday, you're different today")
2. **Archetype language:** Later iterations could blend primary/secondary archetype language into responses (e.g., Flame/Edge user gets slightly sharper language)
3. **Seasonal evolution:** By season 2, Kael's responses could reference season 1 arc ("Remember when you avoided Courage? Now you're living it")
4. **Micro quest pool:** The "Make it smaller" micro quests could use more poetic language to match these responses
5. **Conversational depth:** Some Flame responses could extend to 4 sentences for especially significant quests (Blaze tier)
