## 1. ONBOARDING SCENARIOS — Verdict: Not Good Enough

**The problem:** Current fantasy scenarios (Crossroads, Stranger, Room, Mirror) conflate *aesthetic preference* with *behavioral tendency*. Choosing "silence" tells us someone thinks silence sounds cool — not that they seek spiritual depth. "Say something to the stranger" (Courage) is the obvious "brave" answer, so anyone who *wants* to be brave picks it regardless of actual tendency. We're measuring ideal self, not real self.

**The clinical blind spot:** Someone depressed may not project themselves into fantasy scenarios at all. Anhedonia flattens imagination.

**The counterpoint:** We don't need psychometric validity. We need emotional resonance and a starting hypothesis Kael refines over days 1-7.

### REDESIGNED SCENARIOS: Real-world micro-moments that feel like memories

**Scenario 1: The Saturday**
*"You wake up with nothing planned. No obligations. The whole day is yours. What pulls at you first?"*
- "I want to move — go somewhere, feel something physical." → Body
- "There's something I've been wanting to figure out." → Mind
- "I think about who I could reach out to." → Heart
- "I've been putting something off. Today I could face it." → Courage
- "My space is a mess. I want to get things in order." → Order
- "I just want to sit with the quiet for a while." → Spirit

**Why:** It's behavioral, not aspirational. People recall what they actually do on free Saturdays. Also surfaces depression — someone who thinks "none of these" is giving us signal.

**Scenario 2: The Conversation**
*"A friend tells you something vulnerable — they're struggling. You notice your instinct. What is it?"*
- "I want to fix it. My mind is already looking for solutions." → Mind
- "I feel it in my body — tension, energy, a need to do something." → Body
- "I just want them to feel heard. I lean in." → Heart
- "I want to say the honest thing, even if it's hard." → Courage
- "I start thinking about what they could organize or change." → Order
- "I feel something deeper stir — like their pain connects to something universal." → Spirit

**Scenario 3: The Setback**
*"Something you were counting on falls through — a plan, a job, a relationship. In the first ten seconds, before you think about it, what happens?"*
- "My body reacts first — chest tight, jaw clenched, need to move." → Body
- "My mind starts analyzing — what went wrong, what's the pattern." → Mind
- "I feel the loss of connection more than anything." → Heart
- "Something in me gets defiant — 'fine, watch me.'" → Courage
- "I start making a new plan almost immediately." → Order
- "I go still. I feel the weight of it and try to let it be." → Spirit

**Scenario 4: The Choice** *(adaptive — only shown if signal is ambiguous)*
*"Two paths. One is familiar and safe — you know you'll be okay. One is unknown — it could be better or worse, but you'd learn something about yourself. No one is watching."*
- "The familiar path. I need steady ground right now." → Anchor/Order/Body
- "The unknown path. I need to shake something loose." → Edge/Courage/Spirit
- "I'd stand there for a while, honestly." → Lens/Mind/Well

### THE CHECK-IN (between scenarios 2 and 3)
*"Before we continue — right now, today, how are things?"*
- "I'm doing alright. I want to grow." → functional
- "I'm managing, but something needs to change." → stuck
- "Honestly, I'm having a hard time." → struggling
- "I'm just trying to get through the day." → acute

**This is the spectrum detector.** Embedded in the flow so it doesn't feel clinical. Feels like Kael checking in. Directly feeds the adaptive difficulty system.

### ARCHETYPE REVEAL (after scenarios)
Kael doesn't say "You are The Flame!" like a BuzzFeed quiz. Instead:

> *"I see something in you. There's a pull toward [primary domain] — it's where your energy goes first, even when you don't think about it. And there's [secondary domain] underneath, like a current you might not have noticed yet. I could be wrong. We'll find out together."*

Tentative, curious, human. "I could be wrong. We'll find out together." makes it a hypothesis, not a box.

## 2. THE FIRST QUEST: "The First Stone"

**Constraints:**
- Must be completable by someone lying in bed who cannot get up
- Must create a prediction error (brain expects "set goals" — gets something different)
- Must teach the fog map mechanic immediately
- Must not feel like another habit tracker

### THE FIRST STONE | Ember Tier | ~3 minutes

**Kael's intro:**
> *"Most apps start by asking what you want to fix about yourself. We're not going to do that. Instead, I want you to tell me one thing that's true. Not a goal. Not a resolution. Just something honest about where you are right now. It can be small."*

**Prompt:** *"Right now, the truest thing I could say is..."*

**After submission:** What they typed appears on a stone in the fog map — placed in the world, not logged in a journal. A small area of fog clears. The stone glows faintly.

**Kael's response (adaptive):**

If heavy (sentiment detection):
> *"That took something to say. I'm not going to try to fix it or reframe it. It's there. You said it. That's the first cleared ground on your map. Tomorrow, we build from here — not from some imaginary version of you."*

If lighter/neutral:
> *"There it is. Real. Not a goal, not a fantasy. Just ground truth. Everything we do builds from what's actually true, not from what you think you should be. More map clears tomorrow."*

If very little (1-5 words):
> *"Short and honest. I respect that. The map doesn't need a novel — it needs real ground to build on."*

**Why this works:**
- **Clinical:** Depressed user can type "I haven't left my room in three days." Valid completion. The act of honest self-assessment IS behavioral activation — smallest possible volitional act with real psychological content.
- **Neuroscience:** Strong prediction error. Expected goals, got honesty. Reward is *spatial* (fog clearing) not operant (badges). Exploration rewards don't habituate as quickly as token rewards.
- **Psychology:** This is a self-affirmation exercise disguised as a game mechanic. Writing about honest self-assessment reduces defensiveness and increases openness to change (Sherman & Cohen, 2006).
- **Game design:** Teaches the fog map mechanic immediately. Do things → fog clears → world reveals. Not transactional — exploratory.

## 3. FIRST WEEK — Day by Day

### SPECTRUM CALIBRATION

Based on the check-in, users get one of two tracks:

**Track A: "Growing" (functional or stuck)**
Standard quest difficulty, full 3-slot days by day 3

**Track B: "Steady" (struggling or acute)**
Reduced to 1 quest per day for days 1-3, gentler language, Kael checks in more, no streak pressure

The user never sees these labels. Kael just... adjusts.

---

### DAY 1: "Ground"

**Quest (both tracks): The First Stone** (described above)

**Kael's evening message (if user completed):**
> *"One stone placed. The fog remembers. Sleep knowing that — tomorrow we'll lay another."*

**Kael's evening message (if user didn't complete):**
> Nothing. No guilt. No reminder. Silence is okay on day 1.

**Map state:** 1 tile revealed (center)
**Stats:** +1 to primary domain

---

### DAY 2: "Notice"

**Morning — Kael:**
> *"Today isn't about doing more. It's about noticing one thing that's already happening."*

**Quest — Track A: "The Noticing" | Ember**
*"At some point today, stop what you're doing for 30 seconds. Look around. Name three things you can see, two you can hear, one you can feel. Then go back to what you were doing."*

Completion: Text field — "What did you notice?" (can be one word)

**Quest — Track B: "The Noticing" | Ember (gentler version)**
*"Wherever you are right now, name one thing you can see and one thing you can hear. That's it."*

**Why (Rodriguez):** This is a 5-4-3-2-1 grounding exercise stripped to its minimum. It interrupts default mode network rumination (the neural basis of depressive thinking) without requiring any motivation, movement, or social interaction. The 30-second time constraint makes it feel trivially easy.

**Kael's post-completion:**
Reads their answer and responds with something specific to what they noticed — showing Kael pays attention.

**Map state:** 1 more tile revealed (adjacent to center)
**Stats:** +1 Mind (noticing = awareness)

---

### DAY 3: "Move"

**Morning — Kael:**
> *"Your body has been carrying you through all of this. Today we give it one minute of attention."*

**Quest — Track A: "Sixty Seconds" | Ember**
*"Stand up. Stretch in whatever way feels right — arms up, twist, bend, shake. One minute. No form, no technique. Just move."*

Completion: Tap "Done" — no reflection required.

**Quest — Track A (additional): "The Choice" | Ember (Choice slot)**
Pick 1 of 3 quests matched to secondary archetype domain. Each is Ember tier, under 5 minutes.

**Quest — Track B: "Sixty Seconds" (adapted)**
*"Move your hands. Open and close them, stretch your fingers, make fists. Thirty seconds. Feel the aliveness in them."*

**Why (Chen):** Day 3 is the first body engagement. Track B uses hands specifically because you can do it lying down. The key insight from behavioral activation: we're not asking them to exercise. We're asking them to feel that their body exists and responds to their will.

**Kael's post-completion:**
> *"That's it. Nothing to optimize. You moved because you chose to. That's the whole point."*

**Map state:** 2 more tiles revealed
**Stats:** +1 Body, +1 secondary domain (if Choice completed)

---

### DAY 4: "Speak"

**Morning — Kael:**
> *"Today involves another human being. Just one."*

**Quest — Track A: "The Reach" | Flame**
*"Send a message to someone you haven't talked to in a while. Not 'how are you' — something specific. 'I was thinking about that time we...' or 'Something reminded me of you today.' One message. That's the quest."*

Completion: Self-report ("Done") + optional: paste what you sent or describe how it felt.

**Quest — Track B: "The Thought" | Ember**
*"Think of one person who has been kind to you. You don't have to contact them. Just hold them in your mind for a moment and notice how it feels."*

**Why (Yeager):** Day 4 introduces the social dimension. Track A uses a "micro-dose of vulnerability" — reaching out is a courage act disguised as connection. Track B uses "mental social contact" which research shows activates similar neural circuits to actual social interaction but requires zero social energy expenditure.

**Kael's post-completion (Track A):**
> *"Connection isn't about big gestures. It's about breaking the silence. You just did."*

**Map state:** 2 more tiles revealed
**Stats:** +1 Heart, +1 Courage (Track A) or +1 Heart (Track B)

---

### DAY 5: "Order"

**Morning — Kael:**
> *"Your environment tells a story. Today we edit one sentence of it."*

**Quest — Track A: "One Corner" | Flame**
*"Pick the smallest space that's been bothering you — a drawer, a shelf, your desk, the floor next to your bed. Spend 10 minutes. Not the whole room. Just that corner."*

Completion: Optional photo or "Done" + how it feels to look at it now.

**Quest — Track B: "One Thing" | Ember**
*"Pick up one thing that's out of place and put it where it goes. Just one."*

**Why (Chen):** Environmental control is one of the fastest paths to felt agency. The research on "embodied cognition" shows that physical order in your space reduces cognitive load and increases sense of control. Track B's "one thing" is the absolute minimum viable version of this.

**Kael's post-completion:**
> *"The fog doesn't just live in here. It creeps into your world too. You just cleared some."*

**Map state:** 2 more tiles + first landmark hint visible through fog
**Stats:** +1 Foundation

---

### DAY 6: "Depth"

**Morning — Kael:**
> *"We've been moving outward — body, people, space. Today we go inward."*

**Quest — Track A: "The Mirror" | Flame**
*"Open your Notes app (or a piece of paper). Write this sentence and finish it: 'The thing I keep avoiding thinking about is...' You don't have to share it with me. But you do have to finish the sentence."*

Completion: Self-report ("I did it") — private. Kael doesn't see what they wrote.

**Quest — Track B: "The Weight" | Ember**
*"Close your eyes for 60 seconds. Notice what feeling is most present right now. Name it — even if it's 'nothing' or 'numb.' Opening your eyes is completing the quest."*

**Why (Yeager):** This is the deepest quest so far and it comes on Day 6, not Day 1. By now the user has 5 days of successful completions building self-efficacy. They've earned the right to go deeper. The privacy guarantee ("you don't have to share it with me") is critical — it removes performance anxiety and lets the insight be purely for them.

**Kael's post-completion:**
> *"Going inward takes more courage than going outward. Most people never do it. You just did."*

**Map state:** 2 more tiles + landmark begins to emerge
**Stats:** +1 Depth, +1 Clarity

---

### DAY 7: "The Week"

**Morning — Kael:**
> *"It's been seven days. Not seven perfect days — seven real ones. Let's look at what happened."*

**Quest — "The Clearing" | Ember**
*"Look at your fog map. You have [X] tiles revealed. Each one came from something real you did. Which moment from this week mattered most to you?"*

Completion: Pick one of their completed quests (shown as cards) or write a free response.

**Kael's reflection (adaptive, based on their choice + week data):**
The AI generates a personalized Week 1 summary that:
- Names what they actually did (specific, not generic)
- Notes a pattern ("You showed up 6 out of 7 days — and the day you didn't doesn't erase the ones you did")
- Adjusts archetype hypothesis based on actual quest completion data, not just onboarding
- Previews Week 2 with a hint of what's coming

> *"Here's what I noticed this week: you [specific observation]. Your [primary domain] pulled you forward — but [secondary domain] is where you surprised yourself. Next week, we go deeper into both. The fog has more to show you."*

**Map state:** Landmark fully reveals — named, personal
**Stats:** Bonus to strongest domain + weekly cycle reward (Fragments)

## 4. THE SPECTRUM PROBLEM — Adaptive Difficulty

### Detection (without asking "are you depressed?")

**Signal 1: The Check-In** (during onboarding) — direct but embedded in flow
**Signal 2: Completion patterns** — Track B users who consistently complete within 2 minutes → may be ready for Track A intensity. Users who don't complete for 2+ days → Kael adjusts down.
**Signal 3: Reflection content** — Sentiment analysis on free-text responses.
**Signal 4: Time-of-day patterns** — Completing quests at 3am vs 9am tells us something.

### Adjustment Mechanism

**Never punish non-completion:**
- No streaks that break
- No "you missed yesterday" messages
- Fog doesn't roll back (it holds)
- Kael's tone never implies disappointment

**Gradual escalation:**
- Track B users who complete 5/7 days → next week gets 2-quest days
- Track A users who skip 3+ days → next week simplifies to 1-quest days
- This happens silently — Kael just adjusts

**The "I can't" escape valve:**
On any quest, a "Not today" button. No penalty. Kael says:
> *"The quest will be here if you want it later. Or not. Either way, I'll be here tomorrow."*

**Crisis detection:**
If free-text contains crisis signals → Kael drops all narrative framing → direct compassionate response → crisis resources shown → admin notified.

## 5. IMMEDIATE FELT IMPACT — Neuroscience of "Something Shifted"

**Dr. Rodriguez:**

The three fastest paths to felt agency, ranked by onset speed:

1. **Environmental control** (Day 5's "One Corner") — 10 minutes to visible result. The brain registers "I changed something in my environment" as proof of agency. This is why cleaning feels good when you're stuck.

2. **Social micro-connection** (Day 4's "The Reach") — receiving a response to your message creates reciprocity reward. Takes hours, but the act of sending creates immediate anticipatory dopamine.

3. **Honest self-articulation** (Day 1's "First Stone", Day 6's "Mirror") — the act of naming your experience reduces amygdala activation (affect labeling, Lieberman et al., 2007). Writing "I'm stuck" literally makes you less stuck neurologically.

**What creates "something shifted" vs "I did a task in an app":**

The key is **self-perception shift**. When someone completes a quest, they don't just get points — they get evidence about who they are. "I reached out to someone" → "I'm someone who reaches out." This is self-perception theory (Bem, 1972): we infer our attitudes from our behavior. The quest system must frame completions as identity evidence, not task checkboxes.

Kael's language should always bridge from action to identity:
- Not: "Quest complete! +5 Fragments"
- Yes: "You moved when you didn't have to. That says something about you."

**Avoiding the "new app honeymoon" → abandonment cycle:**

1. **Don't front-load rewards.** Most apps give massive points/unlocks in week 1 then taper off. This trains the brain for diminishing returns. Instead: small consistent rewards that *slightly increase* over time.
2. **Create anticipation, not just reward.** The fog map's "I can see shapes through the fog" creates wanting-to-know, which is neurologically stickier than satisfaction.
3. **Make the guide relationship real.** Kael must reference previous conversations/completions. The moment the user thinks "Kael remembers that" is the retention hook.
4. **Week 2 must feel different from Week 1.** The biggest abandonment risk is "I've seen all the content." Week 2 needs new quest types, new Kael voice registers, and a new fog map region.

## 6. WHAT NOT TO DO — Lessons from Existing Apps

**Habitica's mistake:** Turned everything into XP grinding. Users optimized for points, not growth. Gaming the system IS the system. *Our fix: hide reward amounts until AFTER completion. Never let optimization replace experience.*

**Finch's mistake:** Too cute. The bird is adorable at first but the adults in our demographic (22-35, insight-rich) eventually feel patronized. The aesthetic implies they need hand-holding. *Our fix: Journey-meets-Ghibli is warm without being infantilizing. Kael is a companion, not a pet.*

**Woebot's mistake:** Too clinical. CBT exercises delivered by a bot feel like homework. The therapeutic frame makes users feel like patients, not heroes. *Our fix: Kael never uses therapeutic jargon. No "cognitive restructuring." No "thought records." The same principles, delivered as wisdom, not treatment.*

**BetterHelp's mistake:** Over-promised connection. Users expected a therapist; got a text-based exchange that felt transactional. *Our fix: Kael explicitly says "I'm not a therapist" if asked. Position as guide/companion, never clinical provider.*

**Generic habit trackers' mistake:** Assumed the problem was lack of tracking. It's not. Everyone knows they should exercise. The problem is the gap between knowing and doing. *Our fix: Quests are about the experience, not the metric. We never say "you walked 10,000 steps." We say "you chose to move today."*

**The biggest mistake all apps make:** They treat all users the same. The person who needs to "optimize their morning routine" and the person who hasn't left bed in a week are not the same user. Most apps build for the functional user and accidentally exclude or harm the struggling one. *Our fix: The spectrum system. Track A and Track B. Invisible. Adaptive. Kind.*
