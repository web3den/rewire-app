## WP4 Part A: Ember Completion Responses (36 Total)

Ember quests are the daily foundation. Responses are:
- **Length:** 1-2 sentences (ultra-concise)
- **Tone:** Affirming but grounded, never celebratory
- **Structure:** Acknowledge the completion, subtle forward hook
- **Variability:** 6 domain templates × 6 context variants = 36 responses

### Context Variants (Applied to Each Domain)

1. **First Completion** — They've never completed a quest in this domain
2. **Low Energy** — They completed this quest with energy slider < 33%
3. **High Energy** — They completed this quest with energy slider > 66%
4. **Streak Active** — They're on a 3+ day completion streak
5. **After Skip** — They skipped yesterday but completed today
6. **Standard** — Default context, no special condition

## WP4 Part B: Flame Completion Responses (24 Total)

Flame quests are milestone moments. Responses are:
- **Length:** 2-3 sentences (more developed)
- **Tone:** Personal, acknowledging the SPECIFIC quest, domain-aware metaphor language
- **Structure:** Name what they did → why it mattered → forward pulse
- **Specificity:** Quest-title aware (NOT "You completed a quest" but "You reached across the gap")
- **Variability:** Quest-specific (one per archetypal quest pattern), with context modulation (low energy, streak, first blaze in domain, etc.)

### Flame Quest Categories

Flame quests fall into archetypal patterns tied to how psychological change actually happens:

1. **Reach** — Crossing a relational boundary (messaging someone, asking for help, showing vulnerability to a trusted person)
2. **Move** — Embodied confrontation (trying something physical that scares you, moving toward discomfort in your body)
3. **Build** — Creating order or structure from chaos (organizing a space that represents an internal pattern, establishing a routine that mirrors a commitment)
4. **Witness** — Attending to something difficult without looking away (sitting with a feeling, acknowledging a pattern, listening to your own truth)
5. **Release** — Letting go of something you've been carrying (speaking something unsaid, forgiving, stepping back from a pattern you've been defending)
6. **Root** — Returning to ground after depth exploration (bringing insight into action, translating meaning into choice)
7. **Summon** — Calling forward a quality from inside that feels foreign (acting as your Courage when you're naturally cautious, moving with Vitality when you're naturally still)
8. **Dissolve** — Softening a boundary or rigid belief that's been protective but limiting

For MVP, I'll generate **24 Flame responses** covering these 8 archetypal patterns, with 3 variants each (first Flame in domain, low energy, high energy).

   - Match the archetypal pattern of the completed quest
   - Modulate by energy slider and streak status (A/B/C variants)

3. **Skip flow:** Trigger bottom sheet with 4 options
   - Each option selects from Track A or Track B variant based on user cohort
   - Load the appropriate Micro Quest pool based on "Make it smaller" selection

4. **Notifications:** Queue during off-peak hours (7-9 AM, configurable)
   - Select from daily morning pool most days
   - Trigger milestone notifications at [3, 7, 14, 30] days
   - Trigger dormant mode notifications after 7+ day gap with no completion
   - Never queue multiple notifications without app open in between

## Final Notes

This dialogue pool is the emotional soul of Rewire. Every line should feel like Kael is reading you — not testing you, not cheering you on generically, but *actually seeing what you did and why it matters*.

The responses will be tested, refined, and evolved based on:
- Week 1 user feedback (sentiment analysis on which responses land)
- Engagement metrics (completion rates after different response types)
- Archetype accuracy (do users feel "seen" by domain-specific language?)
- Skip patterns (do Skip Flow responses reduce churn effectively?)

Treat these pools as living, not locked. The voice matters more than the exact words.
